## Abstract
This study investigates how age affects the biological response of mammary gland tissue to spaceflight, focusing on potential risks for breast and ovarian cancers. We introduced a novel bioinformatics pipeline to analyze tissues from young and old mice which were sent to space. Through our analysis, we found that older mice had a significantly stronger molecular response to space flight elements. Key biological processes, such as signal transduction and gene regulation, were observed to be more active in the older group. The molecular changes in older mice showed a stronger association with genes linked to cancer, particularly ovarian and breast cancer. Our analysis points to conclusions that show how age is a critical factor in how the body responds to spaceflight.
